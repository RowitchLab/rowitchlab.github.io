About the Lab

Professor Rowitch is an internationally renowned neonatologist and developmental neuroscientist leading the research enterprise at Cedars-Sinai Guerin Children's. He transitioned to Cedars-Sinai in 2023 while maintaining his leadership positions at the University of Cambridge.

Our laboratory investigates genetic factors that determine glial cell development and diversity, comprising 90% of cells in the human brain. We utilize cutting-edge genomic technologies including whole genome sequencing, single-cell biology, and functional genomics to diagnose and treat rare neurogenetic disorders in children.

As Associate Director of Research at Guerin Children's, Professor Rowitch oversees an ambitious vision to integrate genomic medicine into clinical care, establishing high-impact investigative programs in pediatric research.

Key Research Areas

Glial Cell Biology

Investigating genetic factors determining development and diversity of glial cells, with applications to leukodystrophy and multiple sclerosis

Neonatal Brain Injury

Advanced diagnosis and treatment strategies for white matter injury in premature infants and cerebral palsy

Functional Genomics

Single-cell biology and whole genome sequencing to diagnose rare neurogenetic disorders and develop novel therapies

Regenerative Medicine

Neural stem cell transplantation for conditions like Pelizaeus-Merzbacher Disease and developmental biology research

Newborn Screening Innovation

Developing next-generation screening protocols using biomarker analysis and genomic data for early disease detection

Translational Research

Bridging basic science discoveries with clinical applications to improve pediatric care and outcomes

Five-Year Vision

Our goal is to offer whole genome sequencing to all children admitted to Cedars-Sinai Guerin Children's and eventually expand this capability throughout the Los Angeles healthcare network. We are recruiting a growing team of basic science investigators studying fundamental developmental processes from conception through birth, generating new insights into how genetics impacts development and disease.

Through this integrated approach combining cutting-edge genomics, single-cell analysis, and translational research, we aim to revolutionize pediatric care and establish new standards for precision medicine in children.
