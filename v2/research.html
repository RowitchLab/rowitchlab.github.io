RESEARCH FOCUS

Our Scientific Mission

The Rowitch Laboratory is dedicated to understanding the complex mechanisms that govern chromosome organization and RNA metabolism. Our interdisciplinary approach combines cutting-edge single-molecule techniques, genomics, and computational analysis to address fundamental questions in molecular biology.

Chromosome Dynamics

Investigating the three-dimensional organization of chromosomes and how their spatial arrangement influences gene expression and genome stability.

RNA Regulation

Studying the lifecycle of RNA molecules from transcription to degradation, with a focus on regulatory mechanisms and their implications in disease.

Single-Molecule Methods

Developing and applying advanced imaging techniques to observe molecular processes in real-time, providing unprecedented insights into biological mechanisms.

CURRENT PROJECTS

Research in Progress

Explore our ongoing research projects that push the boundaries of knowledge in molecular biology and genomics

Chromosome Biology

Mechanisms of Chromosome Folding

This project investigates the molecular machinery responsible for chromosome folding and compartmentalization. Using a combination of super-resolution microscopy and chromosome conformation capture techniques, we aim to identify key factors that regulate chromatin structure.

Chromatin 3D Genome Super-resolution Imaging
Learn more about this project
RNA Biology

Dynamics of RNA Processing

We are studying the real-time dynamics of RNA splicing and modification using single-molecule tracking in live cells. This work aims to uncover how these processes are regulated and how their dysregulation contributes to human diseases.

RNA Splicing Single-molecule Tracking Live Cell Imaging
Learn more about this project
Gene Regulation

Epigenetic Control of Gene Expression

This project focuses on understanding how epigenetic modifications influence gene expression patterns during development and in response to environmental cues. We combine genomic approaches with advanced microscopy to study these processes.

TECHNIQUES

Advanced Methodologies

Our laboratory employs state-of-the-art techniques to address complex biological questions

Super-resolution Microscopy

Visualizing cellular structures and molecular processes at nanometer resolution

Single-molecule Tracking

Following individual molecules in real-time to study their dynamics and interactions

Next-gen Sequencing

Analyzing genome-wide patterns of gene expression and epigenetic modifications

In vitro Reconstitution

Recreating biological processes in cell-free systems to study their mechanisms

HIGHLIGHTS

Recent Discoveries

Key findings from our research that advance our understanding of molecular biology

Nature Cell Biology, 2023

Novel Mechanism of Chromosome Compaction

We identified a previously unknown protein complex that plays a critical role in chromosome compaction during mitosis, providing new insights into how cells ensure accurate segregation of genetic material.

Read the publication
Molecular Cell, 2022

Real-time Visualization of RNA Splicing

Using advanced single-molecule imaging, we visualized the splicing process in live cells for the first time, revealing unexpected dynamics and providing new understanding of spliceosome assembly.
